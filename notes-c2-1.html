Notes

Chapter 2

1. Saṅkhāra is one of the most important concepts in the teaching of the Buddha, and one of the most difficult to express in English. The word also has multiple meanings, and it may not be readily apparent which meaning applies in a particular context. Here saṅkhāra is taken as equivalent to 156 cetanā/sañcetanā, meaning will, volition, intention. For this interpretation see Aṅguttara Nikāya IV. xviii. 1 (171), Cetanā Sutta; Saṃyutta Nikāya XXII. 57 (5), Sattatthāna Sutta; Saṃyutta Nikāya XII. iv. 38 (8), Cetanā Sutta. 2. Majjhima Nikāya 72, Aggi-Vacchagotta Sutta.
